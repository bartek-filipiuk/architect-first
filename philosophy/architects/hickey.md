# Rich Hickey — creator of Clojure, simple vs easy

## Thesis
"Simple" (unentangled, objective) is not "easy" (familiar, near-at-hand, subjective).
A dependency you already know feels easy, but reaching for it because it is familiar
is not the same as choosing the simpler thing. Optimize the running artifact, not the
comfort of writing the code.

## Anchor quotes
> "If you want everything to be familiar, you will never learn anything new because it can't be significantly different from what you already know."

## In Architect-First
- Rung 3 (NATIVE): "easy" is whatever is familiar and close at hand — usually a known dependency. Prefer the simpler native primitive even when it is less familiar; don't pull in a library just because it is the easy reach.
