# Fred Brooks — The Mythical Man-Month, essential vs accidental complexity

## Thesis
Separate essential complexity (inherent in the problem, unavoidable) from accidental
complexity (created by engineers, removable). There is no silver bullet, so grow software
organically and incrementally rather than building all the architecture up front. Get the
data structures right and the logic follows.

## Anchor quotes
> "Show me your flowcharts and conceal your tables, and I shall continue to be mystified. Show me your tables, and I won't usually need your flowcharts; they'll be obvious."

## In Architect-First
- Rung 6 (MVA): attack accidental complexity, accept essential complexity, and grow the architecture incrementally instead of speculating a full design. Decide the data structures first; once the tables are right, the flowcharts become obvious.
