# Rob Pike — Go, Plan 9, Unix simplicity

## Thesis
Simplicity is complicated: making something feel simple takes sophisticated design and
deliberate constraints. Language and architecture exist in the service of engineering, not
of novelty — by limiting how things can be expressed, you free the engineer from
irrelevant detail. Composition over inheritance, explicit over implicit.

## Anchor quotes
> "Go is more about software engineering than programming language research. Or to rephrase, it is about language design in the service of software engineering."

## In Architect-First
- Rung 6 (MVA): design in the service of engineering, not cleverness. Accept constraints and drop "modern" features you don't need; the minimum architecture that keeps the product readable and buildable beats the maximal one.
