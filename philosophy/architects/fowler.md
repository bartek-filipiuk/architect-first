# Martin Fowler — ThoughtWorks, evolutionary architecture

## Thesis
Architecture is the set of things that are important and hard to change — so the real
work is deciding what is important, then keeping just those parts healthy. High internal
quality is not a tax; it pays back in weeks, because less cruft sits between you and the
next feature.

## Anchor quotes
> "Architecture is about the important stuff. Whatever that is."

## In Architect-First
- Rung 6 (MVA): minimum viable architecture means protecting the important stuff and leaving the rest free to evolve. Don't gold-plate everything; invest energy where change is costly, and let good internal quality keep delivery fast.
