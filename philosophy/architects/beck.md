# Kent Beck — Extreme Programming, simple design

## Thesis
Design quality is objective, not a matter of taste. The 4 Rules of Simple Design, in
priority order — passes the tests, reveals intention, no duplication, fewest elements —
give a concrete test for "simple": anything that does not serve the first three rules
should be removed.

## Anchor quotes
> "At the time there was a lot of 'design is subjective', 'design is a matter of taste' bullshit going around. I disagreed. There are better and worse designs."

## In Architect-First
- Rung 6 (MVA): the minimum viable architecture is the one with the fewest elements that still passes the tests, reveals intention, and avoids duplication. Strip everything that does not serve those goals — there are objectively better and worse designs.
