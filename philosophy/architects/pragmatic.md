# Dave Thomas & Andy Hunt — The Pragmatic Programmer, DRY & YAGNI

## Thesis
Don't implement something until it is actually needed — speculative flexibility is a form
of technical debt. Every piece of knowledge should have a single, unambiguous
representation; the duplication to avoid is duplication of knowledge, not just of code.

## Anchor quotes
> "YAGNI (You Ain't Gonna Need It)"

> "DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself)"

## In Architect-First
- Rung 1 (YAGNI): if the need is hypothetical, skip it and say so in one line. Build for the requirement in front of you, not for a future that may never arrive — speculative flexibility is debt you pay interest on now.
